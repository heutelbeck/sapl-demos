# SMILE Study Protocol

## Study Overview

**Title:** SMILE -- Strengthening Mental Health of Young People through Innovation and Learning in Europe

**Protocol Number:** SMILE-2025-001

**Funding:** Horizon Europe Grant 101080923

**Study Sites:** Heidelberg (coordinating center), Edinburgh

**Target Enrollment:** 10 participants (5 per site)

## Objectives

**Primary Objective:** Evaluate the efficacy of a gamified CBT mobile application in reducing depressive symptoms in adolescents aged 13-17 as measured by PHQ-9 score reduction from baseline to the end of sequence 3.

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Assess treatment response rate (defined as >= 50% reduction in PHQ-9 total score)
- Monitor adverse events related to the intervention
- Evaluate participant engagement with the gamified application

## Inclusion Criteria

- Age 13-17 years at screening
- PHQ-9 total score between 5 and 19 (mild to moderately severe depression)
- Written informed consent from parent/guardian and assent from participant
- Access to a smartphone compatible with the study application
- Able to attend all scheduled study visits

## Exclusion Criteria

- Active suicidal ideation (PHQ-9 item 9 score >= 2)
- Current use of antidepressant medication
- Diagnosis of bipolar disorder, psychotic disorder, or substance use disorder
- Currently receiving psychotherapy
- Participation in another clinical trial within the past 30 days

## Study Design

This is a multi-site research study conducted across two European sites. All enrolled participants receive the gamified CBT intervention across three sequences over 8 weeks.

## Study Sequences

| Sequence | Timeframe       | Description                                          |
|----------|-----------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| 1        | Weeks 0-2       | Onboarding, baseline assessment, initial app usage   |
| 2        | Weeks 2-4       | Active intervention with guided CBT modules          |
| 3        | Weeks 4-8       | Self-directed intervention with monitoring           |

## Endpoints

**Primary Endpoint:** Change in PHQ-9 total score from baseline to end of sequence 3.

**Secondary Endpoints:**
- Treatment response rate at end of sequence 3
- Time to first treatment response
- Adverse event incidence and severity
- Application usage metrics (sessions per week, minutes per session)

## Visit Schedule

| Visit | Timepoint        | Sequence | Procedures                                              |
|-------|------------------|----------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| V1    | Screening        | --       | Informed consent, eligibility assessment, demographics  |
| V2    | Baseline (Day 0) | 1        | PHQ-9 assessment, app installation, training            |
| V3    | Week 2           | 1        | PHQ-9 assessment, adverse event review, app usage review|
| V4    | Week 4           | 2        | PHQ-9 assessment, adverse event review, app usage review|
| V5    | Week 8           | 3        | PHQ-9 assessment, adverse event review, final evaluation|

## PHQ-9 Scoring

The Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) is a 9-item self-report measure. Each item is scored 0-3. Total scores range from 0 to 27.

| Total Score | Severity          |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 0-4         | Minimal           |
| 5-9         | Mild              |
| 10-14       | Moderate          |
| 15-19       | Moderately severe |
| 20-27       | Severe            |

## Safety Monitoring

All adverse events are recorded and classified by severity (mild, moderate, severe) and causality (unrelated, possibly related, probably related, definitely related). Serious adverse events must be reported within 24 hours. The Safety Officer reviews all adverse events weekly.

## Data Management

Participants are identified by study aliases (P-001 through P-010) in all study data. The alias-to-identity mapping is maintained in the participant registry.
